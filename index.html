Luis Bravo


IT Infrastructure Specialist - FOSS Enthusiast

25 years of experience in IT infrastructure. My expertise covers technologies such as RISC/Intel servers, TCP/IP networks, SAN networks, corporate storage, security, Internet services (DNS, SMTP, WEB, FTP ...) and free software.

At the moment my interests include cloud computing, DevOps and web development.

Some achievements

Datacenter monitoring with Nagios

I deployed the Nagios software to replace an old and obsolete product that began to present problems. Nagios was implanted in a reduced time, using already existing computational and personnel resources, and allowed the monitoring of various equipment and services that were not monitored by the previous solution.

SAP High Availability with PowerHA

I deployed the high availability IBM PowerHA solution in several SAP environments running on AIX. customized the application scripts (Unix shell) to tailor the clusters to the particularities of each environment.

Migration of AIX LPARs

I performed upgrades and migrations of AIX environments on Power machines (P5XX, P7XX) using technologies such as NIM, HCM and PowerVM services. This work was done mainly at times of exchange by newer models, such as the migration of LPARs from P595 to P770 or P780.

Cacti as a capacity planning tool

I deployed the Cacti solution to assist in the server capacity planning process (Unix and Windows). After a process of defining the metrics that would need to be monitored (CPU, memory, IOPS, swapping ...), data collection scripts were implemented for drawing the associated graphics. These graphs have proved very useful both for planning Capacity, as in the diagnosis of environmental performance.

Email filtering with cisco ESA

In conjunction with the IT security department, I established the parameters, developed the RFP, analyzed proposals, and deployed a filtering solution for Internet email. Cisco ESA was the solution adopted. In addition to a reduction in SPAM filtering of more than 90 percent, the solution provided previously unavailable facilities such as traceability and prioritization of messages, content filtering and quarantine of suspicious messages.
